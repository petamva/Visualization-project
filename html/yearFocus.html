2014 releases

The next section is actually more about comparing visualizations rather than extracting information from them. We have collected most of 2014 film and tv-show releases and we present them below in two different ways. A sunburst plot and a tree. Both diagrams are typically used for visualizing hierarchies and networks with connections between nodes and leaves. Here we implement them in a similar way grouping releases in genres, theatrical films and tv-shows. Which one you choose is a matter of personal preference: the sunburst plot is an impressive visualization full with beautiful colors and packed with information. The tree on the other hand is almost an exact opposite: it starts out as a simple small node and as it unfolds only relevant information is displayed making it a minimalistic alter ego of the sunburst diagram.

This interactive data graphic is undoubtedly amazing. But it can also distort information in its attempt to persuade. It is actually quite easy to get overwhelmed by all those flashy colors and the barrage of information. As consumers of data graphics, it behooves us all to arm ourselves with a more thorough language and understanding of data graphics.

It is clear that the tree diagram is following after Edward Tufte's principles: “Graphical excellence consists of complex ideas communicated with clarity, precision, and efficiency”(page.51). After all less is more...
